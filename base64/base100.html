🔍 Base100 Working Principle

  • Character Set: Two-digit numbers 00-99 as 100 symbols (Base100 digits)
  • Encoding Process: Interpret input bytes as large integers, then repeatedly divide by 100 to get digits
  • Formatting: Each digit printed as two digits with leading zeros (e.g., 3→03), usually space-separated
  • Decoding Process: Parse two-digit tokens (00-99), accumulate in Base100, then convert integer to bytes
  • Purpose: This is a text representation for transmission/debugging, not encryption or compression
